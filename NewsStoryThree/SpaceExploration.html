Timeline Overview Of Space Explorations

1969

The US had set their sights on a new endeavor having already lost the space race to the Soviet Union, becoming the first nation to reach the moon. The constant fear of being seen as an inferior nation in terms of technology, led to a new goal set by the US, to send the first man to the moon. A man by the name of Neil Armstrong became the first man to set foot on the moon on July 20th 1969...

1970

The first landing on another planet dates back to 1970, when the soviet union launched the Soviet Venera missions in an attempt to record data from our neighboring planet venus. Although the mission was successful, the results were not what the Soviets expected. A harsh environment inhabitable by humans and technology alike, as the spacecraft could only stay on the surface for one hour before having to return. Although these findings highlighted the harsh reality that space had to offer, it was still a big step in human technology.

1977

In 1977 the US launched voyager 1 and two in an effort to explore deeper into the vastness of space, voyager 1 in particular is still going to this day, exploring deeper and deeper into space until its mission finally comes to an end. The satellites were sent towards Jupiter and Saturn to explore their surfaces, yet the satellites managed to find volcanoes on Jupiter's moon and new discoveries on saturn's rings. The biggest take away from this mission was by far how technology from over 40 years(voyager 1) has had a leading role in helping us discover far into space even today.

1996

On December 4th in 1996 the US Launched the spacecraft named Delta II towards mars in hopes of exploring its surface. The mars pathfinder first made landfall 7 months after the initial launch, becoming the first manmade object to reach the planet. The Pathfinders mission was to analyze the Martian surface, its atmosphere, the climate and geology. The findings suggested that at one point the planet had flowing water, which may have been signs of life for the planet, furthering the belief that we are not alone in the vastness of space.

2020s

Inhabiting Mars has long been the goal for us humans. It is seen as the pinnacle of human achievement in our goal to reach the next level of civilization. There are many things needed for this goal to be possible, yet communication might be the biggest factor in the long run. There will be no knowing how smoothly things are going and whether or not we can send more people to mars without a stable communication network. But these points don't even take into consideration how long it would take to get to mars and back. It would take up to 850 days just to get to mars and back, leaving the question, who is willing to take the risk?, as Being the first also means being a “test subject” for future people to use as an example...

There is still a lot we don't know when it comes to our universe, most of our ignorance comes from our inability to fully explore our surroundings. It is unexplainable how little we know, yet a quote from 1927 said by a man named Arthur Eddington better explained the mystery of our universe, than we ever could “The universe is not only stranger than we imagine, it is stranger than we can imagine”. No matter how much we think we know there is still so much unknown and unexplored. Rather than saying, “we might never fully know what's out there” it is more appropriate to say that we will never fully know what's out there. As the universe is ever expanding, so is our thirst for knowledge and neither will ever reach its end.
