Rising cannabis use Among Norwegian Teens sparks Concerns

Cannabis, commonly known as marijuana, is a versatile plant that has been utilized for various purposes throughout history.


Its primarily known for its psychoactive properties, it contains compounds like THC (tetrahydrocannabinol) and CBD (cannabidiol) that interact with the human brain, resulting in altered perceptions and mood changes. Cannabis is the most widely used illicit dug globally among adolecents and young adultsin several western countries.

Recent findings from prominent surveys highlight a concerning resurgence in cannabis use among Norwegian adolescents, reversing a trend of decline observed since the early 2000s. According to data from the European School Survey Project on Alcohol and Other Drugs (ESPAD) and the Ungdata survey, approximately 9% of Norwegian youths reported cannabis use in 2019, signaling a notable uptick. This rise is particularly pronounced among higher education students, prompting a closer examination of the evolving landscape of cannabis consumption in Norway.

Cannabis in Norway


Norway, traditionally with lower adolescent cannabis use compared to many European counterparts, witnessed a peak in the early 2000s followed by a decline. However, recent data from the European School Survey Project on Alcohol and Other Drugs (ESPAD) and the Ungdata survey hint at a resurgence in cannabis use, reaching approximately 9% among adolescents in 2019. Notably, this increase is echoed in higher education students, signaling a concerning trend that requires further exploration.

Gender Disparities

Analyses across time and regions reveal nuanced patterns. Historically, adolescent boys tend to report higher cannabis use, with potential gender gaps narrowing in recent times. Additionally, variations in cannabis use emerge between urban and rural areas, adding complexity to the understanding of the issue. It's crucial to explore these dynamics to tailor preventive measures effectively.


Implications For Norway’s Youth

The concurrent rise in cannabis use among Norwegian adolescents, alongside global trends, raises critical questions about the effectiveness of existing preventive measures. The need for a comprehensive investigation into age-group-specific, gender-specific, and location-specific data is evident to inform targeted interventions and policies.

Conclusion : What does this all mean?

As cannabis continues to weave its influence globally and locally, addressing the surge in use among Norwegian teens requires a multifaceted approach rooted in understanding evolving trends and tailoring interventions accordingly.
