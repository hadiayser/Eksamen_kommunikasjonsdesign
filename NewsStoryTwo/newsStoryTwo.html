Recent data from the European School Survey Project on Alcohol and Other Drugs (ESPAD) and the Ungdata survey reveals a concerning resurgence in cannabis use among Norwegian adolescents, primarily affecting higher education students.

Approximately 9% of Norwegian youths reported cannabis use in 2019, marking a significant uptick and reversing a previous trend of decline.

Cannabis in Norway


Norway, traditionally with lower adolescent cannabis use compared to many European counterparts, witnessed a peak in the early 2000s followed by a decline. However, recent data from the European School Survey Project on Alcohol and Other Drugs (ESPAD) and the Ungdata survey hint at a resurgence in cannabis use, reaching approximately 9% among adolescents in 2019. Notably, this increase is echoed in higher education students, signaling a concerning trend that requires further exploration.

Chart Analysis: Unveiling Trends in Cannabis Usage



The chart above illustrates key trends in cannabis usage among Norwegian youth, shedding light on crucial turning points over the years.

The chart underlines a significant spike in cannabis usage, nearly reaching a 50% increase in 1999. This period marked a notable shift in the relationship between Norwegian youth and cannabis.

Contrary to the surge in 1999, there was a consistent decline in usage percentages from 1999 to 2011. Despite stringent regulations and presumed preventive measures, the data indicates a steady decrease in cannabis consumption during this period.

However, the trend took an unexpected turn from 2011 to 2019, witnessing a 40% increase in cannabis usage. This shift poses questions about the effectiveness of existing preventive measures and the factors contributing to this resurgence.

Gender Disparities


Analyses across time and regions reveal nuanced patterns. Historically, adolescent boys tend to report higher cannabis use, with potential gender gaps narrowing in recent times. Additionally, variations in cannabis use emerge between urban and rural areas, adding complexity to the understanding of the issue. It's crucial to explore these dynamics to tailor preventive measures effectively.

Interview with a Teen: Understanding the Impact


To provide a firsthand perspective, we spoke with Damien, a 22-year-old student from Oslo. They shared insights into the factors contributing to the rise in cannabis use among Norwegian teens.

Damien's Insights:


"I think one major factor to this is that it is being more commonly talked about & not as looked down upon. For example: TikTok has a trend where people post videos of them having fun while high," Damien noted when asked about the increase in cannabis use.

"Most of my friends haven't ever taken a puff while others who you'd think wouldn't ever do weed need a whole joint to get high," Damien added, shedding light on the varied experiences within their peer group.

"Weed was demonized, and I've seen people claim it to having killed people. The attitude today towards weed is like 'we need to have someone roll a joint for the party tonight.' It's like alcohol, just a milder and safer version," Damien shared regarding the evolving attitudes towards cannabis.
