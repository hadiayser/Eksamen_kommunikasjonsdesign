16 Million Lives: Soviet Union's Post-War Landscape

The devastating toll of the Second World War manifested in a significant decline in the Soviet population. By 1950, the nation witnessed a staggering decrease of almost 15.5 million individuals, marking an eight percent reduction from the pre-war levels of 1940. The largest Soviet state, Russia (RSFSR), bore the brunt of this decline, accounting for almost nine million fewer people, reflecting an identical eight percent decrease. It was a somber reality, and the journey to pre-war population levels was not swift; it took until 1955, a decade after the conflict had ceased.
